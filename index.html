Hormozi Value Equation Analysis
Applied to Q1 2026 performance
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
Value = (Seller's Goal Achieved × Trust in Close) ÷ (Days to Close × Seller Work Required)
Dream Outcome ✅
Q1 closed 33 deals — sellers got cash, certainty, and speed. Novation structure (50%) means sellers received closer to retail pricing. Dream outcome is being delivered.
Perceived Likelihood ⚠
18.2% no-show rate signals trust is not fully established at appointment booking. The seller doesn't believe the meeting is worth attending — pre-frame and social proof need reinforcement.
Time Delay 🔴
Assignment avg close: ~89 days vs 45-day target. Novation avg: ~48 days. Sellers perceive long timelines as risk. UW speed (33 hrs avg) further delays offer certainty. Time delay is the #1 value destructor.
Effort & Sacrifice ✅
As-is purchases, TC handling coordination, no repairs required. This pillar is a competitive strength — the offer structure removes almost all seller effort. Protect this in all deal communications.
🎯 Insight #1 — The Highest Leverage Fix Is Attended → ARIP
At 6.1%, your attended→ARIP rate is below the 8% benchmark. With 1,419 attended appointments in Q1, a 2-point improvement = 28 additional ARIPs. At 70.93% pull-through and 38.4% ARIP→close, that's ~8 extra closes worth ~$297K in revenue — without adding a single new lead or appointment.

Root causes to test: Are AMs presenting 3 exit structures (Pace framework) or just one price? Are offers arriving with complete UW? Is ICP being re-qualified at appointment, or are low-ICP sellers getting meetings?
🔴 Insight #2 — Call Center Volume Is a Q2 Time Bomb
Call Center produced 50% of all ARIP deals in Q1, but volume was 40% of target (1,043 vs 2,600). Revenue hit target because fees were large enough to compensate. But the same-quarter close rate was 15.12% — 85% of closes came from prior pipeline.

Q2 projection: If call center volume stays at 40% of target, the ARIP pipeline will produce ~35 deals vs the 100 target. At current close rates, Q2 revenue would be at risk of falling significantly short. This is a 90-day lag problem starting now.
💡 Insight #3 — Show Rate Recovery Is a $500K+ Opportunity
417 no-shows + 91 cancellations + 21 VP-missed = 529 appointments that didn't produce attended outcomes. Even recovering 30% of no-shows (125 additional attendees) at the current $862 revenue-per-attended-appt = $107K in additional recoverable revenue per quarter.

Fix in order: (1) SMS + email reminder at 24 hrs and 1 hr before every appointment — non-negotiable. (2) Confirmation call by LM same day for all virtuals. (3) ICP gate: no virtual bookings below ICP score 4. (4) VP missed appointments: calendar audit immediately.
📊 Q2 Priority Action Stack
1. Restore Call Center to 200+/wk — audit dialer, lists, and headcount this week.
2. Enforce 3-structure offer presentation on all virtual and IP appointments (Pace framework).
3. Implement confirmation sequence: SMS + email at 24 hrs and 1 hr. Track show rate weekly.
4. UW SLA: 18-hr escalation trigger. No appointment should be attended without complete UW.
5. Onboard Junior TC before Q2 pipeline hits — 37 deals in Under Contract need hands now.
6. Direct Mail restart: scale to 50+ leads/wk. It has the highest ARIP conversion of any channel.
